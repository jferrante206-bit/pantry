How to Use Pantry Tracker
The two-scan system for effortless kitchen inventory
The Core Workflow
1
Scan In — When You Get Home
After shopping, scan each item before putting it away. Tap Add to Pantry on the action sheet. Your stock count goes up automatically.
💡 Batch it — unpack everything first, then scan all at once before putting things away.
2
Scan Out — Before You Toss the Container
When a package is empty and about to hit the trash or recycling, scan it one last time. Tap Mark as Used / Empty. Stock goes down — and if it crosses your reorder threshold, it auto-adds to your list.
💡 The recycling bin is your trigger. Before anything goes in — scan it.
3
Order — From the Grocery List Tab
Your list automatically splits into 📦 Amazon Prime and 🛒 Store / Instacart sections. Hit Copy on either section and paste straight into the app you're ordering from.
💡 For Amazon items, set your reorder threshold high enough to order 2–4 days before you run out.
What Each Scan Action Does
➕
Add to Pantry
Just bought it. Increases your stock count by 1. For first-time items, lets you set category, expiration, and reorder threshold.
📭
Mark as Used / Empty
Decreases stock by 1. If qty hits your reorder threshold, it automatically adds to the right shopping list. If it hits zero, it always adds to your list.
🛒
Add to Grocery List
Adds directly to your shopping list without changing your pantry count. Use when you notice you're low but haven't used the item yet.
🆕
New Item — Set Details
Only appears for items not yet in your pantry. Lets you configure category, expiration date, reorder threshold, and whether to order from Amazon or the store.
Setting Reorder Thresholds

The reorder threshold is the quantity at which the app automatically adds an item to your shopping list — before you run out.

Set it based on how long it takes to restock:

📦 Amazon Set to 3–5 — gives you time to order and receive delivery
🛒 Store Set to 1–2 — you can grab it on your next trip
⚡ Critical Set to 0 to only alert when completely empty

The colored bar on each pantry item shows how close you are to your threshold — green is fine, amber is approaching, red means reorder now.

Tracking Produce

Produce doesn't have barcodes, but it does have PLU codes — the 4–5 digit number on the little sticker.

Use the Quick Produce field on the Scan tab, or tap the Produce tab to browse 50+ pre-loaded codes. You can also add your own.

For produce you use frequently, use the − button manually on the Pantry tab as you cook, rather than scanning each time.

Tips for Sticking With It
🔵 Keep the app on your home screen so it's one tap away — not buried in a folder.
🔵 A Bluetooth scanner ($30–50) sits on the counter and types barcodes automatically — no camera pointing needed.
🔵 Scan in batches. Unpacking groceries and tossing trash are natural moments — do all the scanning at once.
🔵 Adjust thresholds over the first few weeks until the auto-alerts feel right for your rhythm.
🔵 Produce is the exception. Don't stress scanning every banana — just use the − button when cooking.
Reset & Setup
Moving, emptied your fridge, or starting fresh?
Restart the setup wizard to do a fresh scan session. You can choose to clear your existing inventory or keep it and add more items on top.
